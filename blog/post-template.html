How to Export from India: A Beginner's Guide

India's export market is dynamic and full of potential. For a new business, the first and most crucial step is obtaining your Import Export Code (IEC). This ten-digit code is issued by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT).

1. Register Your Business

Start by registering your firm as a sole proprietorship, partnership, or private limited company. Once registered, apply for a PAN card in the firm's name and open a current bank account.

2. Obtain IEC and GST

Apply online via the DGFT portal for your IEC. Simultaneously, register for GST, as it's mandatory for all export operations. Most exports are zero-rated, meaning you can claim a refund on taxes paid on inputs.

3. Product Sourcing & Quality Checks

Identify suppliers who meet international quality standards. Conduct regular site visits and pre-shipment inspections to maintain consistent quality, which is the cornerstone of a successful export trade.
